Super Player
13 years old, Hassabis became world runner-up in chess, and wrote his first artificial intelligence chess program.
In teenage years, Hassabis became addicted to computer games.
17 years old, Hassabis took a gap year. He joined in the Bullfrog Productions,Ltd and began his computer games career. He began first by level designing on Syndicate, and then co-designing and lead programming on the classic computer game , which sold several million copies.
22 years old, Hassabis set up his own game company Elixir Studios. In addition to manage the company, He served as executive designer of the games《Republic:The Revolution》and《Evil Genius》.
Foldit:
A functinal game which allows players to fold the chain of amino acids into the appropriate three-dimensional shape in the form of challenges. It connects scientific questions with the public, and aggregates wisdom through an online platform.
For example, a player once cracked the crystal structure of AIDS reverse transcriptase in 10 days, a scientific puzzle which existed about 15 years.

During Hassabis's postdoctor period in MIT, he was exposed to Foldit, being amazed at the great potential for human intuition to find new patterns in games.
Morning Star——Foldit
Game & AI for Science
35 years old, Hassabis co-founded the artificial intelligence company DeepMind, with the mission of “Use games as a testing ground for proving and opening up AI algorithms”.
In 2016, AlphaGo, a program built under Hassabis's leadership, defeated world champion Lee Sedol.
At that moment, the experience of playing Foldit flashed through his mind.
"If AI can train from 0 knowledge,and beat top human players, it can also do the work of a scientist with 0 knowledge of biochemistry, just like Foldit players."
Since then, the protein folding problem became a key step for DeepMind to move from game intelligence to solving real-world problems.

In December 2020, AlphaFold was launched.
In July 2021, AlphaFold 2 came out.
In May 2024, AlphaFold 3 rolled out.
